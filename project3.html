With data from the World Bank Open Data and John Hopkins University real-time Covid-19 death cases until September 2020, I wanted to see if rich countries, which is defined as high Gross Domestic Product (GDP) per capita and high physicians - hospital bed per 1000 ratio, have lower Covid-19 death rates.

There is no direct relationship between GDP per capita and the ratio of physicians/hospital beds per 1000, as shown in the table below:



Developing countries like Ecuador and Mexico have quite high physicians per 1000 ratios, almost as many as Belgium and the United Kingdom even though these countries have 5 times more income. However, their death rates are just as high as other rich countries. On the contrary, poorer countries such as Afghanistan or Bahrain have much lower death rates.

Of course, there are many other factors to consider to understand the big picture. Here, I just want to know how ready countries around the world were coming into the pandemic. That would be beneficial for officials to implement proper measures, policies and restrictions to deal with the virus.
